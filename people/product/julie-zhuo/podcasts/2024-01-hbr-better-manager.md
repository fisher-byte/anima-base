# How to Become a Better Manager (HBR On Leadership)

## Overview

Julie Zhuo shares her journey from Facebook's first intern to VP of Product Design, offering practical advice for managers struggling with team dynamics. Based on her bestselling book "The Making of a Manager," she addresses specific listener questions about handling underperforming team members and employees who boss others around.

## Key Topics Discussed

### 1. Julie's Management Journey

**The Early Years**:
- Started as Facebook's first intern
- Became a manager in her mid-twenties
- "Often felt like I was in over her head"
- Made "a lot of mistakes" in early management years
- Learned primarily through trial and error

**The Growth**:
- Eventually learned how to manage team dynamics
- Scaled from small team to leading hundreds
- Left Facebook/Meta in 2020 as VP of Product Design
- Now co-founder of Sundial

**Key Learning**: Management skills can be developed - no one is born knowing how to do it well.

### 2. Three Core Areas Every Manager Should Focus On

#### People
- **Hiring**: Bring the right people onto the team
- **Recruiting**: Active talent pipeline development
- **Coaching**: Amplify team members' strengths
- **Performance Management**: Give feedback, help adjust behavior
- **Development**: Help people grow their careers

#### Process
- **Team Coordination**: Ensure everyone works well together
- **Clear Systems**: Define how work gets done
- **Conflict Resolution**: Have processes for handling disagreements
- **Meeting Rhythms**: Establish effective communication patterns
- **Decision-Making**: Clear frameworks for who decides what

Analogy: "If you have an orchestra with the right violinists and percussion players, you still need everyone to know how to work together and play music together."

#### Purpose
- **Shared Understanding**: Everyone knows what success looks like
- **Clear Goals**: Team has same "sheet music"
- **Alignment**: Reduce conflicts from misaligned objectives
- **Scaling**: Becomes MORE important as team grows
- **Context**: People understand the "why" behind their work

### 3. Managing Difficult Team Dynamics

**Scenario 1: Employee Bossing Others Around**

The Problem:
- Individual contributor acting like manager
- Telling others what to do
- Creating confusion about authority
- Undermining actual manager's role

Julie's Approach:
- Have direct, private conversation
- Clarify role boundaries clearly
- Explain impact of their behavior
- Redirect energy into appropriate channels
- Set clear expectations going forward

**Scenario 2: Underperforming Team Member**

The Problem:
- Not meeting expectations
- Affecting team morale
- Unclear if performance can improve

Julie's Approach:
- Start with clear feedback about gap
- Understand root cause (skills, motivation, fit)
- Create specific improvement plan with timeline
- Provide support and resources
- Be willing to make tough call if no improvement
- Being kind doesn't mean lowering standards

### 4. What Great Feedback Looks Like

**Definition**: "Great feedback is simply feedback that changes you in a way that later on you're grateful for and that you believe made your life better."

**Characteristics**:
- **Inspiring**: Motivates change, not just criticism
- **Specific**: Clear about what to change
- **Timely**: Given soon after observation
- **Balanced**: Not just negative - also recognize strengths
- **Actionable**: Person knows what to do differently

**Common Mistake**: Thinking feedback is only about problems. Great managers also give feedback about what's working well.

## Notable Quotes

> "I often felt like I was in over her head and I made a lot of mistakes [as a young manager at Facebook]."

> "If you have talented people and they know how to work together, but they don't have the same picture of what success looks like, you're still going to get conflict."

> "Great feedback is simply feedback that changes you in a way that later on you're grateful for."

> "Purpose becomes more and more important as the organization or the team gets bigger and bigger."

## Practical Takeaways

1. **Everyone starts uncertain**: Even future VPs feel in over their heads early in management career
2. **Focus on three buckets**: People, process, purpose - check daily that you're tending to all three
3. **Be direct but kind**: Have tough conversations early, don't let issues fester
4. **Clarify expectations constantly**: Most team problems come from misaligned understanding
5. **Make feedback inspiring**: Frame it as helping them grow, not just criticism
6. **Purpose scales importance**: The bigger your team, the more critical shared understanding becomes
