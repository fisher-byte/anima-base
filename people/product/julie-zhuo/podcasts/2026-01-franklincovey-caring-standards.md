# Caring Without Lowering the Bar (FranklinCovey On Leadership)

## Overview

Julie Zhuo, former VP of Product Design at Facebook and author of "The Making of a Manager," reflects on the leadership lessons she learned while scaling with one team. She explores the critical balance between caring deeply about your people and maintaining high performance standards—a tension many leaders struggle with.

## Background Context

- **When**: January 27, 2026
- **Host**: Will Houghteling (FranklinCovey)
- **Show**: FranklinCovey On Leadership (Season 51)
- **Julie's Background**: Former VP of Product Design at Facebook, bestselling author, now entrepreneur

## Key Topics Discussed

### 1. The Core Tension: Caring vs. Standards

**The False Choice**:
Many leaders believe they must choose:
- Either be kind and supportive (lowering standards)
- Or maintain high standards (being harsh)

**The Truth**: These are not mutually exclusive

**Julie's Insight**: "Leadership isn't about being nice—it's about being kind, clear, and intentional."

**Difference Between Nice and Kind**:
- **Nice**: Avoiding difficult conversations, making people comfortable
- **Kind**: Honest feedback that helps people grow, even when uncomfortable
- **Nice**: Short-term comfort
- **Kind**: Long-term benefit

### 2. Scaling with One Team at Facebook

**Julie's Unique Experience**:
- Spent 13+ years at Facebook
- Scaled with essentially the same team/organization
- Watched team grow from dozens to hundreds
- Saw same people evolve through different stages

**What Scaling Taught Her**:

**Stage 1: Small Team (10-20 people)**
- Can be close to everyone
- Informal communication works
- High bar feels personal, not systemic
- Direct feedback easier

**Stage 2: Medium Team (50-100 people)**
- Can't know everyone personally
- Need more structure
- Standards must be explicit
- Systems replace personal touch

**Stage 3: Large Team (hundreds)**
- Lead through other leaders
- Culture carries standards
- Clear expectations essential
- Caring becomes institutional

**Key Lesson**: "How you care and how you maintain standards must evolve as you scale, but you never compromise on either."

### 3. Maintaining High Standards While Caring

**The Framework**:

**1. Be Clear About Standards**
- Define what excellent looks like
- Make expectations explicit
- Don't assume people know
- Document and communicate

**2. Explain the Why**
- People accept high standards when they understand purpose
- Connect standards to impact
- Show how it helps them grow
- Make it about the work, not personal judgment

**3. Provide Support**
- High standards + no support = unfair
- Give people tools to meet expectations
- Offer coaching and resources
- Remove obstacles

**4. Give Honest Feedback**
- Kind = telling truth they need to hear
- Not kind = avoiding hard conversations
- Timely feedback shows you care
- Specific guidance helps improvement

**5. Make Tough Decisions When Needed**
- Sometimes caring means helping someone find better fit
- Prolonging poor fit helps no one
- Team suffers when standards drop
- Making hard calls is part of caring leadership

### 4. When Standards and Empathy Collide

**Common Scenarios**:

**Scenario A: Good Person, Wrong Fit**
- Person trying hard but not meeting bar
- Team morale suffering
- Leader feels guilty

**Julie's Approach**:
- Have honest conversation early
- Give clear feedback with timeline
- Provide support to improve
- If no improvement, help them transition
- This is caring for both person and team

**Scenario B: High Performer, Toxic Behavior**
- Delivers results but damages culture
- Team afraid to speak up
- Leader tempted to overlook

**Julie's Approach**:
- Performance alone isn't enough
- Address behavior directly
- Clear expectations for how and what
- No one is above standards
- This is caring for the whole team

**Scenario C: Personal Challenges Affecting Work**
- Team member going through hard time
- Performance slipping
- Leader wants to be supportive

**Julie's Approach**:
- Show empathy for situation
- Offer flexibility where possible
- But be clear about work expectations
- Help them find path forward
- Sometimes that means time off or adjusted role

### 5. Building a Culture of Caring + High Standards

**It Starts with Leadership**:
- Model the behavior you want
- Don't make exceptions for yourself
- Be transparent about decisions
- Show vulnerability when appropriate

**Make It Systemic**:
- Hire for both excellence and values
- Promote people who embody both
- Recognize and reward caring behavior
- Celebrate high achievement with humanity

**Communication is Key**:
- Talk openly about the tension
- Name it when it comes up
- Share how you think about trade-offs
- Make your decision-making process visible

**Long-Term Thinking**:
- "Being nice" feels good now but hurts later
- Being kind feels hard now but helps later
- Short-term discomfort for long-term growth
- Your team will thank you eventually

### 6. Personal Lessons from Facebook Years

**What Julie Got Right**:
- Built culture of high performance with humanity
- Created psychological safety while pushing boundaries
- Developed many leaders from her team
- Maintained relationships even after hard decisions

**What She'd Do Differently**:
- Have hard conversations even earlier
- Be more explicit about expectations sooner
- Trust her judgment on fit issues faster
- Balance work and rest better (for herself and team)

**Key Realization**: "The moments I regret are when I delayed hard conversations to avoid discomfort—both theirs and mine. Being truly caring means having courage to be honest."

## Notable Quotes

> "Leadership isn't about being nice—it's about being kind, clear, and intentional."

> "You can care deeply about people while maintaining high standards. In fact, maintaining high standards IS a form of caring."

> "How you care and how you maintain standards must evolve as you scale, but you never compromise on either."

> "Being 'nice' makes you feel good in the moment. Being kind makes them better in the long run."

> "The moments I regret are when I delayed hard conversations to avoid discomfort."

## Practical Takeaways

1. **Distinguish nice from kind**: Nice avoids discomfort; kind embraces necessary hard truths
2. **Make standards explicit**: Don't assume people know what excellent looks like
3. **Provide support with expectations**: High standards without support is unfair
4. **Have hard conversations early**: Delaying doesn't help anyone
5. **Model both caring and standards**: Your team watches what you do more than what you say
6. **Think long-term**: Short-term discomfort for long-term growth
