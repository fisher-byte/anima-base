# Design Better Podcast: Facebook's former VP of Product Design on what she got right and wrong as a leader (Rewind)

## Overview

This is a rewind episode from 2019, re-aired in July 2024. Julie Zhuo reflects on her journey from Facebook's first intern to VP of Product Design, sharing personal examples of what she got right and wrong as a leader. The episode covers the difference between leading and managing, and provides practical advice for advancing your career.

## Background Context

- **When**: Originally aired 2019, rewind episode July 30, 2024
- **Julie's Role**: Former VP of Product Design at Facebook (now Meta)
- **Current**: Co-founder of Sundial (data-driven decision-making platform)
- **Book**: Author of "The Making of a Manager" (2019 bestseller)

## Key Topics Discussed

### 1. Early Career Journey

Julie started as:
- Facebook's first intern (~employee 100)
- Grew with one of the fastest-growing companies on the planet
- Eventually led product design for the Facebook app for many years
- Left Facebook in 2020 after 13+ years

### 2. Leadership vs Management

**Management**:
- It's a role/job with specific responsibilities
- Can be given to you or taken away
- Goal: help your team achieve great outcomes

**Leadership**:
- It's a quality, not a role
- Someone others want to follow
- Takes initiative and rallies people toward solutions
- Can be a manager or non-manager
- Great managers should exhibit leadership qualities

### 3. Early Management Challenges

- Felt "in over her head" in her mid-twenties
- Made many mistakes early on
- Learning happened through trial and error
- Eventually learned how to manage team dynamics
- Scaled from small team to leading hundreds

### 4. Building Top-Notch Design Teams

Julie became renowned for building excellent teams by focusing on:
- Hiring and developing great people
- Creating effective processes
- Maintaining clear purpose and vision

## Notable Quotes

> "Management is a role; it's a job... it can be given to you, it can be bestowed to you, and it can be taken away from you."

> "Leadership is not a role. Leadership is a quality. Leaders can be managers or they can be non-managers."

> "I often felt like I was in over my head and I made a lot of mistakes [as an early manager]."

## Practical Takeaways

1. **Distinguish roles from qualities**: Understanding that management is a job but leadership is a skill helps clarify your development path
2. **Embrace early mistakes**: Everyone struggles when starting as a manager - it's part of the learning process
3. **Focus on team building**: Hiring right and developing people is the foundation of good management
4. **Grow with your company**: Julie's story shows how starting early at a high-growth company can accelerate learning
