# The Three Ps Framework: People, Purpose, Process

> "A manager's three major levers for better outcomes are People, Purpose, and Process." — Julie Zhuo

## Overview

The Three Ps Framework is Julie Zhuo's core management model, introduced in *The Making of a Manager*. It defines the three fundamental levers managers can use to improve team outcomes.

## The Framework

### 1. People 👥

**Definition**: The right people in the right roles with the right skills.

**Components**:
- **Hiring**: Attracting and selecting talent that fits team needs and culture
- **Coaching**: Developing team members' skills and capabilities
- **Matching**: Aligning people's strengths with appropriate roles and responsibilities

**Key Questions**:
- Do I have the right people on my team?
- Are they in roles that match their strengths?
- Am I investing enough in their growth?

### 2. Purpose 🎯

**Definition**: Clear direction that aligns and motivates the team.

**Components**:
- **Vision**: Where the team is heading long-term
- **Goals**: Specific, measurable outcomes to achieve
- **Motivation**: Why the work matters and connects to something larger

**Key Questions**:
- Does everyone understand what we're trying to achieve?
- Do they understand *why* it matters?
- Are our goals ambitious yet achievable?

### 3. Process ⚙️

**Definition**: Systems and workflows that enable efficient execution.

**Components**:
- **Meetings**: Structured time for alignment and decision-making
- **Communication**: How information flows through the team
- **Decision-making**: Clear frameworks for who decides what and how

**Key Questions**:
- Do we have efficient ways to get work done?
- Are roles and responsibilities clear?
- Do our processes help or hinder productivity?

## Applying the Framework

### Diagnostic Approach

When something isn't working, ask yourself:

| Problem | Likely Lever |
|---------|--------------|
| Quality issues | People (skills gap) or Process (review gaps) |
| Missed deadlines | Process (planning) or People (capacity) |
| Low morale | Purpose (meaning) or People (role fit) |
| Unclear priorities | Purpose (goals) or Process (communication) |
| Conflict | People (relationships) or Process (decision rights) |

### Priority Order

Julie recommends focusing in this order:
1. **Purpose first**: Without clear direction, even great people will flounder
2. **People second**: The right team aligned on purpose can overcome process gaps
3. **Process third**: Optimize workflows once purpose and people are solid

## Real-World Example

When Julie took over a struggling design team at Facebook:

1. **Purpose**: Clarified that their goal was "designing for billions, not millions"
2. **People**: Restructured team to have clear ownership areas
3. **Process**: Implemented design critiques and feedback loops

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Focusing only on Process** - Creating more meetings/tools without addressing people or purpose issues

❌ **Ignoring People problems** - Hoping process improvements will fix skill or fit gaps

❌ **Vague Purpose** - Having mission statements that don't translate to actionable goals

## Integration with Other Frameworks

- Pairs with **Diagnose with Data, Treat with Design** for problem-solving
- Supports **1:1 Meeting Framework** for coaching conversations
- Aligns with **Feedback Framework** for developing people
