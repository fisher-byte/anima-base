# Shopify's AAA Framework

> A neat framework for clarifying responsibilities on projects. It's called the AAA framework.

## Overview

Shopify's product team uses the AAA framework to eliminate confusion about who does what on cross-functional projects. This simple but powerful tool reduces ambiguity, prevents duplication of work, and ensures clear ownership.

## What AAA Stands For

### 1. Approver - The Decision Maker

**Role**: The person who has final authority to say "yes" or "no" to decisions within their domain.

**Responsibilities**:
- Makes final decisions
- Takes accountability for outcomes
- Provides clear direction
- Sets priorities and expectations
- Owns the outcome

**Example**: Engineering Approver makes final call on technical implementation decisions

**Key rule**: There should be only ONE approver per domain to avoid conflicting decisions.

### 2. Accountable - The Owner

**Role**: The person who owns the project end-to-end and ensures it gets done.

**Responsibilities**:
- Drives the project forward
- Coordinates between teams
- Tracks progress and deadlines
- Raises blockers early
- Communicates updates and risks

**Example**: PM is accountable for ensuring feature ships on time with quality

**Key rule**: Every project needs ONE accountable person who wakes up thinking about it daily.

### 3. Actionable - The Contributor

**Role**: The person who does the work and delivers results.

**Responsibilities**:
- Executes specific tasks
- Provides input and expertise
- Delivers on commitments
- Raises issues they encounter
- Collaborates with team members

**Example**: Designer is actionable for creating mockups and user flows

**Key rule**: Multiple people can be actionable on different aspects of a project.

## How to Use AAA in Practice

### Scenario: Launching a New Feature

**Step 1: Assign roles at project kickoff**

```
Feature Launch: Checkout Redesign

Approver:
- Product Director: Makes go/no-go decision on launch
- Engineering Manager: Approves technical approach and timeline

Accountable:
- Senior PM: Owns the entire project from start to finish

Actionable:
- Senior Designer: Creates designs and mockups
- Frontend Lead: Implements user interface
- Backend Lead: Builds API and data models
- QA Lead: Writes and runs tests
```

**Step 2: Make roles visible**
- Put AAA assignments in project documentation
- Reference in meetings when questions come up
- Update when ownership changes

**Step 3: Follow the framework**
- Decisions go through the Approver
- Status updates come from the Accountable
- Work happens through Actionable team members

## Common Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### ❌ Multiple Approvers
**Problem**: Conflicting decisions, slow decision-making
**Fix**: Establish clear domains with one approver each

### ❌ No Accountable Owner
**Problem**: Work falls through cracks, finger-pointing
**Fix**: Always assign ONE person who owns the project

### ❌ Unclear Who's Actionable
**Problem**: Duplicate work, missed tasks
**Fix**: Explicitly call out who's doing what

### ❌ Role Confusion
**Problem**: Wrong people making decisions or doing work
**Fix**: Reference AAA when questions arise

## Benefits of AAA Framework

### 1. **Faster Decision-Making**
Clear approvers eliminate the "let me check with..." loop
- Single point of decision
- No waiting for consensus
- Clear escalation path

### 2. **Accountability & Ownership**
One accountable person ensures projects don't stall
- Someone "wakes up thinking about it"
- Clear who to follow up with
- No "I thought someone else was doing that"

### 3. **Reduced Ambiguity**
Everyone knows what they're responsible for
- Less time clarifying roles
- Fewer duplicate tasks
- Better collaboration

### 4. **Better Cross-Functional Collaboration**
Clear roles prevent stepping on toes
- Designers design, engineers engineer
- PMs coordinate, approvers approve
- Respect for expertise boundaries

## When to Use AAA

**✅ Perfect for**:
- Cross-functional projects
- Launch planning
- Product initiatives
- Process changes
- Team collaborations

**⚠️ Adapt for**:
- Small 2-person projects (can be overkill)
- Clear individual work (no delegation needed)
- Emergency fixes (speed trumps process)

## Implementation Tips

1. **Assign AAAs early** - Set roles at project kickoff, not when issues arise
2. **Make it visible** - Put AAA in shared docs, project trackers, meeting agendas
3. **Reference it regularly** - Use AAA language when questions come up
4. **Update as needed** - Roles can change, but always have AAA defined
5. **Train the team** - Ensure everyone understands the framework

## Why This Matters

The biggest source of friction in product development is unclear roles and responsibilities. AAA solves this elegantly with:

- **Simplicity**: Three roles everyone understands
- **Clarity**: No ambiguity about who does what
- **Accountability**: Single owners prevent buck-passing
- **Speed**: Clear approvers enable fast decisions

This framework helps teams move faster, with less drama, and with better outcomes.

## Related Frameworks

- **RACI**: More complex alternative (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed)
- **DACI**: Decision-oriented framework (Driver, Approver, Contributor, Informed)
- **RASCI**: Extended RACI with Support role

AAA is often preferred in fast-moving product environments for its simplicity and focus on decision-making and ownership.
