# Three Types of Product Managers

> A framework to understand the three most common types of product managers

## Overview

This thread breaks down the three distinct archetypes of product managers that exist in the industry, helping both hiring managers and aspiring PMs understand where they fit and how to build balanced teams.

## The Three Types

### 1. Builders 🏗️

**Who they are**: PMs who excel at shipping new features, launching products, and getting things done.

**Key characteristics**:
- Strong execution skills
- Pragmatic problem solvers
- Comfortable with ambiguity
- Focus on shipping and learning
- Excellent at breaking down complex problems

**When they shine**:
- Early-stage startups
- 0→1 product development
- Fast-paced environments
- Feature launches

**Watch out for**:
- May prioritize speed over strategy
- Can build the wrong thing efficiently
- Sometimes skip discovery work

### 2. Tuners ⚙️

**Who they are**: PMs who excel at optimizing existing products, improving metrics, and driving incremental gains.

**Key characteristics**:
- Analytical and data-driven
- Patient with experimentation
- Comfortable with optimization loops
- Focus on metrics and iteration
- Strong A/B testing skills

**When they shine**:
- Mature products
- Scale and optimization phases
- Growth optimization
- Metric-driven environments

**Watch out for**:
- May over-optimize on local maxima
- Can miss bigger opportunities
- Sometimes get stuck in analysis paralysis

### 3. Innovators 💡

**Who they are**: PMs who excel at finding new opportunities, challenging assumptions, and driving breakthrough ideas.

**Key characteristics**:
- Creative and visionary
- Strong product sense
- Pattern recognition skills
- Comfortable with high uncertainty
- Excellent at customer insight

**When they shine**:
- New market exploration
- Product strategy development
- Innovation initiatives
- Competitive disruption

**Watch out for**:
- May struggle with execution details
- Can generate too many ideas
- Sometimes skip validation

## Key Takeaways for Hiring

**Don't look for one "perfect" PM** - look for the right type for your stage:

- **Early stage**: Prioritize Builders
- **Growth stage**: Mix of Tuners + Builders
- **Mature stage**: Mix of all three, with Tuners often leading
- **Pivots/innovation**: Innovators become critical

**Balance is key**: The best product teams have a mix of all three types. They complement each other's blind spots.

**Self-awareness matters**: Understanding your type helps you:
- Choose the right opportunities
- Play to your strengths
- Identify areas to develop
- Communicate your value effectively

## For Aspiring PMs

**Know your natural strength**:
- Do you love shipping? You might be a Builder
- Do you love optimization? You might be a Tuner
- Do you love new ideas? You might be an Innovator

**Build complementary skills**:
- Builders: Learn strategy and data analysis
- Tuners: Learn discovery and customer insight
- Innovators: Learn execution and experimentation

**Honest self-assessment** helps you find teams where you'll thrive and roles that match your strengths.

## Why This Framework Matters

Many hiring frustrations come from mismatched expectations:
- Hiring a Tuner for 0→1 work (won't move fast enough)
- Hiring a Builder for optimization work (won't be systematic enough)
- Hiring an Innovator for execution-heavy work (won't deliver consistently)

Understanding these types helps both sides set the right expectations and build better, more balanced product teams.
