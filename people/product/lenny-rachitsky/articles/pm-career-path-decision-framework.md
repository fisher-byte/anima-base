# PM Career Path Decision Framework

> When people ask me where they should try to go work, I encourage them to find a company that (1) is best at teaching them the craft of product management and (2) has a track record of creating an inflection in the careers of PMs who've worked there.

## Overview

**Lenny's Career Philosophy**: Choose company for growth, not just prestige.

**Two criteria for career decisions**:
1. **Company teaches PM craft** well
2. **Company accelerates PM careers** (creates inflection points)

**Why this matters**: A company might be prestigious (FAANG, unicorn) but terrible at developing PMs. Or a smaller company might be incredible at growing PMs.

---

## The Career Ladder Framework

### Understanding PM Career Structures

**Common confusion**: PM role is inconsistently defined and evaluated across companies.

**Reality**:
- Two-thirds of companies have both IC (Individual Contributor) and Manager tracks
- You can switch between IC and Manager tracks (usually at L6/Sr. PM level)
- Titles vary wildly by company
- Skills/competencies evaluated differ by company

**The decision challenge**: How to navigate career path when standards differ?

### Typical Career Ladder Structure

**Most companies follow** this pattern (variations exist):

| Level | Title | Track Options | Key Competencies |
|-------|--------|-------------:|------------------|
| L1/L2 | Associate PM | IC | Learning execution, basic PM skills |
| L3/L4 | Product Manager | IC | Independence, problem-solving, stakeholder management |
| L5/L6 | Senior Product Manager | IC | Strategic thinking, cross-functional leadership |
| L6 | Senior Product Manager | Manager | People management, team building |
| L7/L8 | Staff/Distinguished PM | IC | Company-wide impact, mentoring |
| L8+ | Principal/Vice President | Manager | Org leadership, strategy |

**Key decision points**:
- **L6 (Sr. PM)**: Decide IC vs. Manager track
- **L7+ (Staff/Principal)**: Decide breadth vs. depth

## Decision: IC vs. Manager Track

### The Switch Decision

**When to choose Manager track**:
- You enjoy developing people
- You want to scale impact through team
- You're good at hiring and coaching
- You're comfortable with people problems

**When to choose IC track**:
- You want to stay close to product/users
- You enjoy deep problem-solving
- You don't want to manage people
- You want to become recognized expert

**Lenny's insight**:
> "Two-thirds of companies have both an IC track and a manager track. When there's a manager track, you can switch at L6 (i.e., Sr. Product Manager)."

**Decision rule**: Switch is **reversible** (can try, go back). Test it before committing permanently.

### The "Super IC" Decision

**From Tal Raviv (via Lenny)**

**What is "Super IC"?**
- Senior IC role (Principal/Distinguished PM)
- Company-level impact without people management
- Deep product expertise, mentoring, strategy

**When to pursue Super IC**:
- You're excellent at craft
- You want deep impact over broad
- You enjoy mentoring, not managing
- You want to avoid politics

**Companies that support Super IC**:
- Clear levels and titles (Principal PM, Distinguished PM)
- IC compensation competitive with management
- Recognition for IC contributions

## Decision: Which Company to Join

### The Career Acceleration Framework

**Lenny's research**: Which companies create inflection in PM careers?

**Top companies for PM career acceleration** (from Lenny's analysis):

| Company | Why It Accelerates | Types of Growth |
|---------|-------------------|------------------:|
| **Stripe** | Rapid growth, strong PM culture, mentorship | IC → Manager → VP |
| **Figma** | Craft excellence, product-led culture | Deep IC skills → Strategic leadership |
| **Airbnb** | Scale experience, complex problems | PM → PM → Growth → Product leader |
| **Google** | Depth of expertise, systematic growth | PM → Sr PM → Principal PM |
| **Meta (Facebook)** | Rapid iteration, big impact | PM → PMM → Director |

**Decision criteria for company choice**:

**Factor 1: Craft Teaching**
- Does company have strong PM culture?
- Are there senior PMs to learn from?
- Is product decision-making clear?
- Are career ladders transparent?

**Factor 2: Career Inflection**
- Do PMs at this company grow faster?
- Are there examples of PMs who started here and advanced?
- Is there room for growth?
- Are internal mobility opportunities common?

**Factor 3: PM vs. PMM Ratio**
- What's the ratio of Product Managers to Product Marketing Managers?
- (PMM = Marketing, not PM track—ensure you're joining right role)
- Some companies misrepresent roles

### Company Research Decision Framework

**Before joining**, investigate:

**Ask in interviews**:
- "What's the career path for PMs here?"
- "Can you show me the career ladder?"
- "How do PMs get promoted here?"
- "What's the typical growth trajectory?"
- "Who are examples of PMs who've advanced here?"

**Research online**:
- LinkedIn: Look at current/former PMs and their trajectories
- Glassdoor/Blind: Review career progression feedback
- Company blog/careers page: Look for development resources

**Talk to current/former employees**:
- "How did you grow here?"
- "What's the PM culture like?"
- "What's the career advancement process?"
- "Would you recommend this company for PM career growth?"

## Decision: When to Move Jobs

### The Stay vs. Go Framework

**When to stay**:
- You're still learning (gaining skills, exposure)
- You have clear growth path (next level achievable)
- You have good manager who advocates for you
- You're working on high-impact projects
- Compensation is fair/growing

**When to move**:
- You're not learning (stagnating skills)
- No clear growth path (dead-end role)
- Bad manager/no advocacy (blocked advancement)
- Low-impact work (not advancing career)
- Compensation below market

**The "2-Year Rule"**:
- Minimum 2 years in role before moving
- Exception: Toxic environment or misrepresentation

**Lenny's insight from community**:
> "Community Wisdom: The demise of the PM role at Airbnb, how [PM role shifted]"

**Lesson**: Roles evolve. Don't stay because of loyalty if role no longer develops you.

## Decision: Skills to Prioritize

### The PM Skills Framework

**From Lenny's research on what separates junior from senior PMs**

**Core skills by level**:

**Junior PM (L1-L3)**:
- Execution (ship features reliably)
- Problem-solving (simplify complex requests)
- Communication (write specs, facilitate meetings)
- Stakeholder management (coordinate with eng, design, data)
- Data literacy (read analytics, interpret trends)

**Senior PM (L4-L6)**:
- Strategic thinking (prioritize, roadmapping)
- Cross-functional leadership (influence without authority)
- User research (design and conduct studies)
- Business acumen (understand market, competition)
- Mentorship (develop junior PMs)

**Staff/Principal PM (L7+)**:
- Company-wide impact (initiatives affecting entire org)
- Deep product expertise (recognized authority in domain)
- Org design (structure teams, processes)
- Strategic direction (shape product vision)
- Industry influence (speaking, writing, community)

### Decision: Which Skill to Develop

**Prioritization matrix**:

| Skill | Your Strength | Career Stage Impact | Company Need | Decision Priority |
|--------|-------------|------------------:|--------------|----------------:|
| Strategic thinking | ⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ✅ Top priority |
| User research | ⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐ | ✅ High |
| Data analysis | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ✅ High |
| Stakeholder management | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐ | ⚠️ Medium |
| Technical depth | ⭐ | ⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⚠️ Medium |
| Public speaking | ⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐ | ❌ Low |

**Decision rule**: Prioritize skills where:
- Your strength is weak (biggest growth opportunity)
- Career stage impact is high (leverage next promotion)
- Company has significant need (make you visible)

## Decision: Career Timing

### When to Seek Promotion

**Readiness criteria**:
- You've been in role 18+ months
- You're performing at next level (exceeding current level expectations)
- Your manager advocates for you (explicit support)
- You've mentored/supported others (leadership)
- You've driven high-impact initiatives (clear wins)

**The promotion conversation**:
1. Discuss with manager (3+ months before)
2. Align on gaps (what you need to demonstrate)
3. Create plan (specific projects, metrics)
4. Execute plan (demonstrate performance)
5. Formal request (with evidence of next-level performance)

### When to Switch Tracks

**IC → Manager**:
- You've mastered IC role (3+ years at L6+)
- You've demonstrated leadership (leading projects, mentoring)
- You enjoy developing people
- There's manager opening (or can create one)

**Manager → IC**:
- You're not enjoying people management (after trying it)
- You want to return to product focus
- You're at L6+ (level where switch is typical)
- IC path exists at your company (or you're willing to move)
