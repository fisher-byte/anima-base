# PMF Decision Scenarios - Lenny's Framework Collection

> Product-market fit decisions are the most critical product decisions. How you navigate uncertainty, measure progress, and choose between optimization vs. expansion determines startup trajectory.

## Overview

PMF (Product-Market Fit) isn't a binary yes/no—it's a continuum you gradually improve. These frameworks help Lenny make data-driven PMF decisions across multiple companies.

**Core decision scenarios covered**:
1. **Finding PMF** - How to search systematically
2. **Measuring PMF** - Quantifying progress with 40% Rule
3. **Positioning at PMF** - Focusing on single attribute (Rahul Vohra's approach)
4. **Pre-PMF prioritization** - What to build when nothing works
5. **Post-PMF scaling** - When to expand vs. optimize

---

## Scenario 1: Finding PMF - Systematic Search

### The Challenge

In pre-PMF stage, you have:
- Uncertainty about user needs
- Limited resources
- Competing feature ideas
- No clear growth engine

### Lenny's Framework: B2B PMF Guide

**Core approach**: Start by making 10 users very happy, not chasing all features

#### Step 1: Choose the Right Problem

**Decision criteria**:
- Is it painful enough? (People mention it daily)
- Are they willing to pay to solve it?
- Is current solution bad enough to inspire change?
- Is it big enough to support business?

**Avoid**:
- Nice-to-have features (non-critical)
- Problems that are "hard to solve" vs. "urgent to solve"
- Solutions that don't require urgency

#### Step 2: Deep User Engagement

**Don't just ask what they want**. Instead:

**Observe their work**:
- How do they currently solve this problem?
- What tools are they using?
- Where are their workarounds?
- What frustrates them?

**Dig for pain**:
- "What keeps you up at night?"
- "What task do you dread most?"
- "What would you pay to never do again?"

**Understand goals**:
- Not what features they request
- What are they trying to accomplish?
- What does success look like for them?

#### Step 3: Rapid Iteration

**Speed > quality in early stage**:

```
Build minimum useful thing → Get real feedback → Adjust → Repeat
```

**Key decisions in iteration**:
- Keep or pivot? (Based on retention + passion)
- Narrow or expand? (Based on segment satisfaction)
- Add features or remove? (Based on actual usage)

**Decision rule**: If small segment shows strong retention, double down there. Don't expand to other segments yet.

#### Step 4: Focus on Right Metrics

**Early-stage metrics (what matters)**:
- **Retention rate**: Are they coming back? (Day 1/7/30)
- **Usage depth**: What features do they actually use?
- **NPS/referral rate**: Do they recommend it?

**Ignore**:
- Raw user numbers (vanity metric)
- Conversion rate (not enough data)
- Growth rate (before you've found PMF)

**Decision framework**: If retention is improving and NPS is positive → You're on track. If retention is flat or declining → Revisit problem/solution fit.

### Case Example: Finding PMF in B2B

**Scenario**: Early B2B startup with multiple feature ideas

**Approach**:
1. **Identify high-value segment** (not biggest segment)
2. **Interview 10 customers deeply** (not 100 shallow interviews)
3. **Build one thing exceptionally well** (not three things adequately)
4. **Measure retention daily** (not monthly)
5. **Iterate weekly** (not quarterly)

**Key insight**: Small number of delighted users > large number of lukewarm users.

## Scenario 2: Measuring PMF - The 40% Rule Engine

### The Framework (Rahul Vohra)

**Core question**: "How would you feel if you could no longer use [Product]?"

**PMF benchmark**:
- **< 40%**: Not at PMF yet
- **40%+**: Reached PMF, can accelerate growth
- **50%+**: Strong PMF, ready to scale

**Source**: Sean Ellis survey of 100+ successful startups

### Decision: When to Apply 40% Rule

**Decision criteria**:
- Minimum 30-40 active users surveyed
- Within last 2-4 weeks (not all-time users)
- Include users with actual experience (not just trials)

**Frequency**: Monthly or quarterly to track changes

### Decision: What the % Means

#### For segments scoring 40%+

**Decision: Double down**
- Deepen understanding of this segment
- Optimize for their needs
- Stop building features for other segments
- Target marketing to this ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

**Questions to answer**:
- What do they have in common?
- What features do they value most?
- Where do they hang out? (for distribution)

#### For segments scoring < 40%

**Decision: Ignore or deprioritize**
- Don't build features for them yet
- Don't market to them
- Don't optimize product for them
- Focus efforts on better-performing segments

**Rationale**: You can't make unhappy users happy. Don't waste resources trying.

#### For segments in the middle

**Decision: Test and learn**
- Interview to understand what's missing
- A/B test small improvements
- Monitor if % is trending toward 40%
- Prepare to pivot if not

### Decision: When You've "Found" PMF

**Rahul Vohra's experience**:
> "When we hit PMF, we started feeling pull. Users started finding us."

**Decision indicators**:
- Natural growth (referrals bringing new users)
- Retention is strong even with product flaws
- Pricing power emerges (can increase price without churn)
- Competitors start copying

**At this stage**:
**Decision: Transition from finding PMF → expanding PMF**

- Optimize acquisition channels
- Scale sales organization
- Build adjacent features
- Enter adjacent markets

**Key distinction**: Don't confuse "pull" (finding PMF) with "push" (scaling after PMF).

## Scenario 3: Positioning at PMF - Rahul Vohra's Single Attribute

### The Decision: What to Position Around

**Rahul Vohra's decision for Superhuman**:
> Position around **single attribute: speed**
> Ignore most customer feedback, manually onboard every new user, obsess over every detail

**Why this decision worked**:
- Clear differentiation in crowded market (email clients)
- Targeted ideal customers (high-frequency email users)
- Focused product development (don't build everything)
- Measurable attribute (can optimize for speed)

### Decision Framework: Choosing Positioning Attributes

**Criteria for single attribute**:
1. **Is it truly important** to target segment?
2. **Can you be best in the world** at it?
3. **Is it measurable** and optimizable?
4. **Does it create defensibility**?

**Superhuman's decision matrix**:

| Attribute | Importance | Competitive Advantage | Feasibility | Decision |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|-----------|:--------:|
| Speed | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ✅ Double down |
| Price | ⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐ | ❌ Don't lead with |
| Integration | ⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ❌ Build later |
| Features | ⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐ | ❌ Remove noise |
| Mobile | ⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐ | ❌ Eventually |

### Decision: What to Ignore

**Rahul's counterintuitive decision**: Ignore most customer feedback

**Framework for filtering feedback**:
1. **Is this feedback from our ICP?** (high-frequency, speed-obsessed users)
   - **Yes**: Consider seriously
   - **No**: Acknowledge but deprioritize

2. **Does this feedback align with our positioning?** (speed)
   - **Yes**: Build it
   - **No**: Explain we're focused on speed

3. **Does improving this enhance our single attribute?**
   - **Yes**: Prioritize
   - **No**: Defer

**Rationale**: If you say yes to everything, you dilute your positioning and become mediocre at everything.

### Decision: Manual Onboarding Strategy

**Decision**: Every new user gets onboarded manually by the CEO

**Cost**: High time investment, doesn't scale
**Benefit**: Deep understanding of user needs, product issues, competitive landscape

**Decision criteria**:
- When to stop manual onboarding?
  - When you understand patterns
  - When you have documented playbook
  - When 80%+ of issues are predictable

- How to transition?
  - Gradually hand to onboarding team
  - Maintain regular CEO onboarding sessions (10% of users)
  - Use manual sessions to train the team

**Learning from this decision**:
- CEO understands product at depth
- Insights flow directly to product decisions
- Manual process gets automated eventually

## Scenario 4: Pre-PMF Prioritization - What to Build

### The Challenge

Pre-PMF, you're navigating:
- High uncertainty about what users want
- Competing hypotheses about what to build
- Limited runway
- Need to validate quickly

### Lenny's Framework: Confidence Meter + LNO

**From Shreyas Doshi (via Lenny)**

#### LNO Framework for Prioritization

**Three types of work**:
- **L (Low-hanging fruit)**: Quick wins, visible impact
- **N (New opportunities)**: Innovation, differentiation, new growth
- **O (Optimization)**: Improving existing value

**Decision rule**: Maintain balance across L-N-O categories

**Example decision matrix**:

| Initiative | Type | Impact | Confidence | Decision |
|-----------|-----|--------|-----------|:--------:|
| Fix critical bug | L | High | 10/10 | Do now |
| Add social features | N | Medium | 4/10 | Test with 5 users |
| Improve onboarding flow | O | High | 8/10 | After critical bugs |
| Explore new market | N | Unknown | 3/10 | Skip for now |
| Optimize database | O | Medium | 7/10 | In 2 sprints |

**Key decision**: Pre-PMF, bias toward **L** and **N** to find PMF, not **O** (optimizing product that doesn't have fit).

### Decision: Confidence Meter Scoring

**For each idea**, rate 1-10 on:
- User validation (interviews, surveys)
- Problem clarity
- Market signals
- Execution capability
- Technical risk

**Decision thresholds**:
- 1-3: Don't pursue yet
- 4-6: Consider with caution, test small
- 7-8: Prioritize and build
- 9-10: Top priority, full investment

**When to pursue**:
- Confidence 7+ AND
- Alignment with positioning AND
- Clear user segment AND
- Fits within runway

**Example**: Adding social features to email client
- Confidence: 4/10 (users haven't asked for it)
- Alignment: Low (positioning is speed)
- Decision: Don't build, deprioritize

## Scenario 5: Post-PMF Scaling Decisions

### The Challenge

After finding PMF, new decisions emerge:
- Scale vs. optimize
- New segments vs. deepen existing
- Adjacent markets vs. adjacent features
- Build vs. buy/acquire

### Decision Framework: Expansion Readiness Checklist

**Before expanding, verify**:
- ✅ Current segment retention > 50% (strong PMF)
- ✅ Organic growth rate > 20% (viral coefficient)
- ✅ CAC/LTV ratio < 0.33 (3-month payback)
- ✅ Product can serve 10x current users (infrastructure ready)
- ✅ Team can support growth (hiring/training ready)

**If 3+ check boxes**: Ready to expand
**If 1-2 check boxes**: Optimize current PMF first
**If 0 check boxes**: You may not have PMF yet—revalidate assumptions

### Decision: Channel Selection for Growth

**Lenny's growth channel framework**:

**Classic channels** (weakening in AI era):
- Inbound marketing (SEO, content)
- Outbound sales (cold email, calling)
- Virality (referrals, social sharing)
- Events (conferences, meetups)
- Lifecycle marketing (upsell, cross-sell)

**Emerging channels** (AI era):
- Ecosystem/partnerships (distribution via other platforms)
- ChatGPT/integrated apps (distribution via AI tools)
- Product-led growth (viral within product)

**Decision framework for channel selection**:

| Channel | Our Advantage | CAC Estimate | LTV Potential | Decision |
|---------|-------------|------------:|------------|:--------:|
| Content marketing | Strong (Lenny's audience) | Low | High | ✅ Double down |
| Paid ads | Medium | High | Medium | ⚠️ Test, not scale |
| Partnerships | High (product fit) | Low | High | ✅ Prioritize |
| Cold outbound | Low | High | Low | ❌ Skip |
| Viral features | Unclear | Low | Very High | ✅ Build into product |

**Decision rule**: Become world-class at **one** channel that leverages your advantage, before adding more.

## Lenny's PMF Decision Philosophy

### Core Principles

1. **PMF is quantifiable** - Not a feeling, but data
   - Use 40% rule systematically
   - Track trends, not snapshots
   - Combine quantitative + qualitative

2. **Focus before expansion** - Small delighted segment > large lukewarm audience
   - Don't optimize for non-ICP users
   - Say no to non-aligned opportunities
   - Build depth, not breadth

3. **Measure to learn, not to prove** - The survey is for product decisions
   - Ask "what disappointed you?" not "do you like it?"
   - Track changes over time, not single data point
   - Use insights to guide product, not validate ego

4. **Positioning simplifies decisions** - Single attribute > unclear differentiation
   - Best at one thing > good at everything
   - Clear positioning guides feature prioritization
   - User self-selects based on positioning

5. **PMF is a journey, not destination** - Continuous improvement
   - < 40% → 40%+ is first milestone
   - 40%+ → 50%+ is second milestone
   - Always optimizing, never "done"

### Decision Patterns

**Pre-PMF decisions**:
- What problem to solve? (Search)
- What segment to target? (Focus)
- What to build first? (Prioritize)
- How to measure progress? (PMF score)
- Should we pivot? (40% trend)

**At-PMF decisions**:
- How to scale acquisition? (Channel selection)
- Which segments to expand? (ICP refinement)
- What adjacent features to build? (Cross-sell)
- How to optimize growth engine? (CAC/LTV)
- When to enter new markets? (Geographic/vertical)

**Scaling decisions**:
- How to build moat? (Differentiation)
- What to build vs. buy? (Make/buy decision)
- How to organize team? (Structure)
- What to automate? (Efficiency)
- When to raise capital? (Runway timing)

## Common PMF Decision Pitfalls

### ❌ Premature Expansion

**Problem**: Expanding to new segments before current PMF is strong

**Signals you're not ready**:
- PMF score flat or declining
- Customer acquisition churn > 30%
- Team struggling with support load
- No clear ICP profile

**Fix**: Double down on current segment until 40%+ → 50%+

### ❌ Feature Creep

**Problem**: Building features for users who don't contribute to PMF

**Detection**:
- Features requested by users scoring < 40%
- Features that don't increase retention
- Features not aligned with positioning

**Fix**: Use LNO framework—prioritize L (core users) and N (innovation), defer O for non-ICP users

### ❌ Chasing Vanity Metrics

**Problem**: Optimizing for users who don't stay

**Vanity metrics** (ignore):
- Total signups
- Page views
- Social media followers
- One-time downloads

**Retention metrics** (optimize):
- Day 1/7/30 retention
- Active user ratio
- Core feature usage
- Organic growth rate

### ❌ Premature Optimization

**Problem**: Optimizing product before validating users want it

**Rahul Vohra's insight**:
> "Early on I had PMF anxiety. There isn't really a moment when that changed. We've just been growing fairly consistently."

**Fix**: Use confidence meter—don't optimize (O) until confidence is high (7+)

### ❌ Ignoring Feedback from ICP

**Problem**: Dismissing feedback from best customers

**Detection**:
- Users scoring 50%+ giving negative feedback
- Competitors stealing your best users
- Churn among high-frequency users

**Fix**: Immediately investigate—even if it contradicts your roadmap. These are your most important users.

## Quick Reference: PMF Decision Flow

```
Start
    ↓
[Decision: Is problem urgent enough?]
    ↓ Yes
[Decision: Are people willing to pay?]
    ↓ Yes
[Decision: Can we solve it better than alternatives?]
    ↓ Yes
Build MVP → Deploy to 10 users → Measure retention
    ↓
[Decision: Retention improving?]
    ↓ No
    ↓ Yes
[Decision: PMF score trending up?] → [Decision: Is PMF score > 40%?]
    ↓ Yes             ↓ No
[Decision: Optimize ICP]   [Decision: Pivot or kill]
    ↓
[Decision: Can we scale?]
    ↓ Yes
[Decision: Single attribute positioning?]
    ↓
[Decision: What channel to double down?]
    ↓
[Decision: What adjacent opportunity?]
    ↓
Scale acquisition → Optimize growth → Expand market
```
