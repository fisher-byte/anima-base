# Aligning your Positioning & the Myth of Product Market Fit

## Episode Overview

April Dunford, positioning consultant and author of "Obviously Awesome," challenges conventional wisdom about Product Market Fit and discusses how to align positioning across the entire organization. This conversation dives deep into why PMF is a problematic concept and how positioning can drive real business results.

## Key Topics Discussed

### 1. **The Origin Story of "Obviously Awesome"**

**Why Self-Published:**
- Traditional publishers were horrified by positioning as a topic
- "Too niche," "not sexy enough," they said
- Self-publishing gave April complete creative control
- The book became a bestseller anyway, proving publishers wrong

**What Happened When She Asked a Real Author for Advice:**
- Shocking response: "Your book is too tactical and actionable"
- Traditional business books are often vague and philosophical
- April deliberately made hers practical and immediately useful
- Result: Became essential reading for product and marketing teams

### 2. **Why Product Market Fit Doesn't Exist**

**April's Controversial Take:**
April argues PMF is a "VC pipe dream" that isn't operationally useful:

**Problems with PMF Concept:**
1. **Too Binary**: You either have it or you don't—no nuance
2. **Not Actionable**: What do you DO if you don't have it?
3. **Ignores Positioning**: Same product, different positioning = different "PMF"
4. **VC-Centric**: Useful for investment decisions, not operations

**Better Framework:**
- Focus on positioning clarity instead
- Measure specific metrics (conversion, retention, NPS)
- Recognize that different segments may have different levels of fit

**Real Example:**
April shares a story from early in her career:
- Same product, repositioned from "Enterprise CRM" to "CRM for Investment Banks"
- Sales went from terrible to great overnight
- Product didn't change—positioning did
- Did PMF suddenly appear? No, positioning aligned with the right market

### 3. **The Problem with "Fill in the Blanks" Positioning Statements**

**Why They Don't Work:**
- Too simplistic for complex products
- Force artificial structure
- Don't capture competitive context
- Result in generic statements

**Example of a Bad Template:**
"We help [customer] do [thing] so they can [benefit]"
- Doesn't address: Who are your competitors?
- Doesn't show: Why you vs. alternatives?
- Doesn't explain: What makes you different?

**Better Approach:**
April's methodology focuses on:
- Understanding competitive alternatives first
- Identifying unique differentiated capabilities
- Mapping capabilities to value
- Finding best-fit customers
- Choosing the right market category

### 4. **Positioning Against Actual Competition**

**Not Just Direct Competitors:**

Most companies think too narrowly about competition:
- **Direct competitors**: Other companies selling similar products
- **Indirect competitors**: Different solutions to the same problem
- **Status quo**: Doing nothing, using spreadsheets, manual processes

**Example:**
If you sell project management software:
- **Direct**: Asana, Monday.com, Jira
- **Indirect**: Email + spreadsheets, meetings
- **Status quo**: Sticky notes on a wall

**Key Insight:**
Often your biggest competitor isn't another product—it's the customer's current (imperfect) solution.

### 5. **Market Point of View (POV) Story**

**What is a POV Story?**
A narrative that:
- Frames the problem from your perspective
- Explains why traditional approaches fail
- Shows why your differentiation matters
- Guides customers to value your unique strengths

**Structure of a Good POV Story:**
1. **The Old Way**: Here's how people traditionally solve this problem
2. **The Problem**: But that approach has these limitations
3. **The Shift**: The world has changed in these ways
4. **The New Way**: Our approach addresses these new realities
5. **The Proof**: Here's why this matters and evidence it works

**Purpose:**
- Helps customers understand your value proposition
- Aligns internal teams on messaging
- Differentiates you from competitors
- Makes your positioning stick

**Example Application:**
- Use in sales pitches to set context
- Include in marketing materials to educate
- Share in onboarding to align new hires
- Reference in product roadmap discussions

### 6. **Creating Organizational Alignment**

**Why Alignment Matters:**
Without positioning alignment:
- Sales describes products inconsistently
- Marketing targets wrong customers
- Product builds features nobody asked for
- Customer success doesn't know how to measure success

**How to Create Alignment:**

**Step 1: Cross-Functional Workshop**
Include representatives from:
- Sales (frontline perspective)
- Marketing (messaging expertise)
- Product (capability knowledge)
- Customer Success (usage insights)
- Leadership (strategic vision)

**Step 2: Collaborative Positioning Exercise**
Work through April's 5-component framework together:
1. Map competitive alternatives
2. List differentiated capabilities
3. Connect capabilities to value
4. Identify best-fit customers
5. Choose market category

**Step 3: Build the POV Story**
Create a narrative everyone can use:
- Sales can adapt for pitches
- Marketing can expand for campaigns
- Product can reference for roadmap
- CS can use for onboarding

**Step 4: Socialize and Iterate**
- Test with customers
- Gather feedback from teams
- Refine messaging
- Document learnings

### 7. **How to Use POV to Win Against Competitors**

**The Strategy:**
Don't just compete on features—reshape how buyers think about the problem

**Techniques:**

**A. Reframe the Category**
Instead of "We're a better CRM," say:
"CRMs were built for sales teams. But investment banks need something different..."

**B. Highlight Your Strengths**
POV story should naturally lead customers to value YOUR differentiators:
- If you're faster → POV emphasizes speed mattering
- If you're simpler → POV shows complexity is the real problem
- If you integrate better → POV explains why integrations matter

**C. Make Alternatives Look Incomplete**
Not by trash-talking, but by:
- Showing the full picture of what's needed
- Explaining why partial solutions fail
- Demonstrating comprehensive approach

**Example:**
"Traditional tools focus on project management. But modern teams need project management + collaboration + documentation + workflow automation—all in one place. Switching between tools wastes 2 hours per person per day..."

### 8. **Involving Sales in Positioning**

**Why Sales Must Be Involved:**
- They talk to customers daily
- They hear objections and competitive comparisons
- They know what resonates and what falls flat
- They're the positioning frontline

**What Sales Can Contribute:**
- Real customer language (not marketing jargon)
- Competitive intelligence
- Deal win/loss patterns
- Objection handling insights

**How to Involve Sales:**
- Include sales reps in positioning workshops
- Interview sales about common customer questions
- Review sales call recordings
- Test new positioning with sales first

**Sales Enablement:**
Once positioning is set:
- Train sales on the POV story
- Provide talk tracks and examples
- Create battle cards vs. competitors
- Role-play customer conversations

## Practical Frameworks Discussed

### Positioning Workshop Agenda

**Duration**: Half-day workshop

**Participants**: Cross-functional team (6-10 people)

**Agenda**:
1. **Frame the Challenge** (30 min)
   - Why are we here?
   - What problems are we seeing?

2. **Map Competitive Alternatives** (45 min)
   - What would customers use instead?
   - What are they using today?

3. **Identify Differentiation** (60 min)
   - What makes us unique?
   - What can we do that alternatives cannot?

4. **Connect to Value** (45 min)
   - So what? Why does each differentiator matter?
   - What problems does it solve?

5. **Define Best Customers** (45 min)
   - Who cares most about our value?
   - What characteristics do they share?

6. **Choose Market Category** (45 min)
   - What category showcases our strengths?
   - Where can we win?

7. **Build POV Story** (60 min)
   - Craft the narrative
   - Test internal alignment

8. **Next Steps** (30 min)
   - How will we socialize this?
   - When will we revisit?

## Key Quotes

> "Product Market Fit is a VC concept, not an operational one."

> "Positioning isn't something marketing does TO sales—it's something the whole company does TOGETHER."

> "The best positioning makes your differentiation obvious without you having to spell it out."

> "Sales teams have the most valuable insights about positioning—they're talking to customers every day."

> "A good POV story doesn't just describe your product—it reshapes how customers think about the problem."

## Action Items for Product Leaders

1. **Stop Using PMF as a Goal**: Replace with specific, measurable objectives
2. **Run a Positioning Workshop**: Get sales, marketing, and product in a room
3. **Develop Your POV Story**: Frame the problem to highlight your strengths
4. **Test with Customers**: Does your positioning resonate?
5. **Enable Your Sales Team**: Give them the tools to position effectively
