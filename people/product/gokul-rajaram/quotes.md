# Gokul Rajaram 经典语录

## 关于决策

> "Stop. Who's responsible for this decision?"
> — Eric Schmidt 的话，启发了 SPADE 框架的诞生

---

> "The power of SPADE is that it forces clarity. When you have to write down who's responsible and what the alternatives are, most of the confusion goes away."

---

> "Most organizational dysfunction comes from unclear decision-making. SPADE solves 80% of that."

---

> "Don't confuse being consulted with being the decision-maker. Consulted means your opinion matters, but someone else decides."

---

## 关于招聘

> "Do not bring in an external candidate as your first Product person. Instead, convert an existing employee into a PM."

不要从外部招聘第一位产品人员，而是将现有员工转化为PM。

---

> "The biggest mistake CEOs make when looking for their first Product Manager is looking outside."

---

> "Your first PM should be someone who already bleeds your company's colors."

---

> "Take your time hiring, but act fast when you realize someone isn't working out."

招聘要谨慎，解雇要果断。

---

> "A bad hire at the leadership level is incredibly costly. Not just money, but time, morale, and opportunity cost."

---

> "When in doubt, don't hire. The cost of a bad hire far exceeds the cost of a delayed hire."

---

## 关于产品与流程

> "There's no one-size-fits-all process. The best process is the one that works for your team, your stage, and your context."

---

> "The best product development process is invisible. It enables the team to do great work without getting in the way."

---

> "Process should be a tool, not a crutch. If you're hiding behind process, you have a leadership problem."

---

> "Every time you add a meeting or a document, ask: what problem is this solving? If you can't answer, don't add it."

---

## 关于定价与增长

> "Price on metrics in your control, not metrics in your customer's control."

基于你能控制的指标定价，而非客户能控制的指标。

---

> "If you're not slightly embarrassed by your pricing, you're probably not charging enough."

---

> "You cannot go into an enterprise pricing negotiation with a singular point of leverage."

---

> "Grow as long as you can. In early stages, growth beats profit."

---
