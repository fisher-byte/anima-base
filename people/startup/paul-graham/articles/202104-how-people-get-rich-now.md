# How People Get Rich Now

Every year since 1982, *Forbes* magazine has published a list of the richest Americans. If we compare the 100 richest people in 1982 to the 100 richest in 2020, we notice some big differences.

## The Shift from Inheritance to Creation

**In 1982**: The most common source of wealth was inheritance. Of the 100 richest people, **60 inherited from an ancestor**. There were 10 du Pont heirs alone.

**By 2020**: The number of heirs had been cut in half, accounting for only **27 of the biggest 100 fortunes**.

### Why Fewer Heirs?

Not because inheritance taxes increased—in fact, they decreased significantly during this period. The reason the percentage of heirs has decreased is not that fewer people inherit wealth, but that **more people are creating it**.

## The Rise of Technology Founders

How are people creating new fortunes? **Mainly by starting companies.** Of the 73 new fortunes in 2020, 56 came from founders' stakes in companies.

And what kind of companies? **Mostly technology companies.** The technological revolution has made it possible to create enormous amounts of wealth much faster than before.

### Key Changes

1. **Technology scales differently**: Software and internet businesses can grow far faster than traditional businesses
2. **Lower barriers to entry**: You don't need a factory or massive capital to start
3. **Global markets**: Technology companies can reach billions of users
4. **Winner-take-most dynamics**: Network effects create enormous winners

## What This Means

This shift from inheritance to creation is actually **good news** for society:

### More Meritocratic
Wealth increasingly comes from what you build, not who your parents were. This is more fair and more motivating.

### More Dynamic
Created wealth means:
- Innovation and progress
- New products and services
- Economic growth
- More opportunities for others

### More Competitive
When fortunes come from starting companies, there's always room for new players. You don't need to be born into wealth to become wealthy.

## The Role of Technology

**Why now?** The acceleration in founder-created wealth isn't random. It's driven by technology, which has changed the economics of starting companies:

- **Lower costs**: Cloud computing, open source, digital distribution
- **Faster growth**: Internet distribution, viral growth, network effects
- **Bigger markets**: Global reach from day one
- **Higher valuations**: Investors understand the power law of tech returns

## Implications for Inequality

Does this mean inequality is good? Not necessarily. But it's important to understand that:

1. **The nature of inequality has changed**: More people creating wealth vs. fewer people inheriting it
2. **Creation is different from extraction**: Building valuable companies helps others
3. **Mobility has increased**: More possible to go from nothing to billions

The question isn't whether some people should be able to get very rich, but whether they're getting rich by creating value or extracting it.

## The Path Forward

For anyone thinking about how to build wealth today:

**The path is clearer than ever**: Start a company that creates something people want. The tools, knowledge, and opportunities are more accessible than at any time in history.

The data shows that **this is how people get rich now**—not through inheritance, not through financial engineering, but through building companies that create value.

---

**Core Takeaway**: The source of great wealth has shifted from inheritance to entrepreneurship, primarily in technology. This is a more meritocratic and dynamic system, where wealth comes from creating value rather than inheriting it.
