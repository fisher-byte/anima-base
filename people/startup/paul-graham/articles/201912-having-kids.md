# Having Kids

Before I had kids, I was afraid of having kids. Up to that point I felt about kids the way the young Augustine felt about living virtuously. I'd have been sad to think I'd never have children. But did I want them now? No.

## The Fear

**If I had kids, I'd become a parent, and parents, as I'd known since I was a kid, were uncool.** They were dull and responsible and had no fun. And while it's not surprising that kids would believe that, to be honest I hadn't seen much as an adult to change my mind. Whenever I'd noticed parents with kids, the kids seemed to be terrors, and the parents pathetic harried creatures, even when they prevailed.

## The Reality

Having kids turned out to be **nothing like I expected**. The experience was:
- More interesting than I anticipated
- Less constraining than I feared
- More rewarding than I imagined

## What Changed My Mind

### 1. Kids Are Interesting
**Kids aren't just small, incomplete adults.** They're exploring the world for the first time, and watching them do that is endlessly fascinating. Everything is new to them. Their reactions to things are often surprising and insightful.

### 2. You Grow With Them
**You don't suddenly become a parent and then stay that way.** You start with an infant (relatively easy) and gradually work your way up. By the time they're teenagers, you've had years of practice.

### 3. The Trade-offs Are Worth It
**Yes, having kids constrains your life.** But in exchange you get:
- Unconditional love (both ways)
- A front-row seat to human development
- A sense of meaning and purpose
- Moments of transcendent joy

## The Unexpected Parts

### Kids Are Funny
Not in a "cute things kids say" way (though that too), but genuinely funny. They have a different perspective on the world, and it's often hilarious.

### You Learn More
**Teaching kids forces you to really understand things.** When a 4-year-old asks "why?" you can't give a bullshit answer. You have to actually know.

### Time Becomes More Valuable
**Having kids makes you more productive with the time you do have.** When your time is scarce, you use it better.

## The Hard Parts

**It's not all upside.** Kids are:
- Expensive
- Time-consuming
- Sometimes frustrating
- Always demanding

But here's the thing: **these downsides are real, but they're outweighed by the upsides** in ways that are hard to explain to someone who hasn't experienced it.

## The Paradox

**Having kids is the classic case of something you can't explain to someone who hasn't done it.** Before you have kids, all you see are the constraints. After you have kids, you wonder why you waited.

This isn't because you've been brainwashed by hormones (though hormones help). It's because the experience fundamentally changes what you value.

## The Advice

If you're on the fence about having kids:

**Don't overthink it.** If you wait until you feel "ready," you'll never feel ready. There's never a perfect time.

**Trust the process.** Yes, it will change your life. But it will change your life in ways that make the changes feel worth it.

**Don't wait too long.** The biological window is real, and it closes. If you think you might want kids someday, "someday" should probably be soon.

## What I Wish I'd Known

**I wish someone had told me that having kids would make me happier.** Not in the moment-to-moment sense (though sometimes that too), but in the deep, lasting sense of having done something meaningful.

The conventional wisdom about parenting emphasizes the sacrifices. And there are sacrifices. But **what no one tells you is how much you get in return**.

---

**Core Takeaway**: Before having kids, you only see the constraints and downsides. After having kids, you discover rewards and meaning that completely change the calculation. The experience is fundamentally different—and better—than it appears from the outside.
