# How to Get New Ideas

The way to get new ideas is to notice anomalies: what seems strange, or missing, or broken? You can see anomalies in everyday life (much of standup comedy is based on this), but the best place to look for them is at the frontiers of knowledge.

## The Fractal Nature of Knowledge

Knowledge grows fractally. From a distance its edges look smooth, but when you learn enough to get close to one, you'll notice it's full of gaps. These gaps will seem obvious; it will seem inexplicable that no one has tried x or wondered about y. In the best case, exploring such gaps yields whole new fractal buds.

## Finding Anomalies

**At the frontiers**: The best place to notice anomalies is at the edge of what's known. When you're learning cutting-edge material, you're more likely to spot:
- Missing pieces that should exist
- Contradictions between theories
- Patterns that don't quite fit
- Questions no one has asked

**The obviousness paradox**: When you find a good gap at the frontier, it will seem obvious that someone should work on it. The reason no one has is usually that:
1. Most people haven't gotten close enough to notice it
2. The gap requires combining knowledge from multiple domains
3. It seems too hard or tedious (schlep blindness)

## Requirements for Generating Ideas

**Deep knowledge**: You need to learn enough about a field to get close to its frontiers. Surface-level knowledge won't reveal the interesting gaps.

**Curiosity**: You have to actually wonder about things. Many people learn facts without asking "why?" or "what if?"

**Independence**: You need to be willing to pursue ideas that seem strange to others, because the best gaps often look unpromising at first.

## From Anomalies to Ideas

Noticing an anomaly is the first step. The next is asking:
- Why does this gap exist?
- What would filling it require?
- What would become possible if we filled it?

Often the answer to "why does this gap exist?" reveals either a hard technical problem (good) or a fundamental impossibility (back to the drawing board).

## Everyday vs. Frontier Anomalies

**Everyday anomalies**: Comedians make a living noticing odd things about daily life. These anomalies can yield business ideas (things that are unnecessarily difficult or annoying).

**Frontier anomalies**: These are found at the edge of human knowledge. They're more likely to yield breakthrough discoveries or technologies, but require more specialized knowledge to see.

---

**Core Takeaway**: New ideas come from noticing gaps and anomalies at the frontiers of knowledge. The key is to learn enough to see these gaps, and to maintain the curiosity and independence to pursue them when others don't.
