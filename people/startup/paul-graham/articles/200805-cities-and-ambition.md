# Cities and Ambition

Great cities attract ambitious people. You can sense it when you walk around one. In a hundred subtle ways, the city sends you a message: **you could do more; you should try harder**.

## Different Messages from Different Cities

The surprising thing is how different these messages can be.

### New York
**New York tells you, above all: you should make more money.** There are other messages too, of course. You should be hipper. You should be better looking. But the clearest message is that you should be richer.

### Cambridge (Boston)
**What I like about Boston (or rather Cambridge) is that the message there is: you should be smarter.** You really should get around to reading all those books you've been meaning to.

### Silicon Valley
**The Bay Area message is: you should be more powerful.** Not in the traditional sense of commanding people, but in the sense of being able to change the world.

## How Cities Send Messages

When you ask what message a city sends, you sometimes get surprising answers. As much as they respect brains in Silicon Valley, the message the region sends is not "you should be smarter." It's "you should be doing something important."

**The message is ambitious, but it's also somewhat narrow.** If you're not working on the biggest problems, you're not measuring up. This can be both motivating and exhausting.

## Choosing Your City

Which message you want to hear depends on who you are. Some people thrive under one message and wither under another.

**The danger of the wrong city**: If you're in the wrong place, you're constantly swimming against the current. Not just because other people's goals aren't your goals, but because the **ambient message of the place** isn't aligned with what you care about.

### Matching Message to Work

- **If you want to make money**: New York or London
- **If you want to do intellectual work**: Cambridge, Berkeley, or certain neighborhoods
- **If you want to build technology companies**: Silicon Valley
- **If you want to make art**: Varies by medium (visual art → New York, film → LA)

## The Power of Ambient Messages

Cities send messages that are:
- **Implicit**: You absorb them without realizing
- **Constant**: They affect you every day
- **Powerful**: They shape what you value and how you measure yourself

This is why the question "**what does this city tell me to do?**" is so important. You'll be hearing that message every day, whether you want to or not.

## Resisting the Message

Can you just ignore the ambient message? **Not really.** You can try, but it's like trying to ignore the weather. It affects you whether you acknowledge it or not.

Better to choose a place whose message you want to hear.

## Beyond the Big Three

Most ambitious people should probably move to one of the big three: New York, Silicon Valley, or Cambridge. But there are niches:
- Film → Los Angeles
- Music → varies by genre
- Politics → Washington DC
- Specific industries → specialized cities

## The Danger of Staying Put

One of the biggest mistakes ambitious people make is **staying in the place where they grew up**, if that place's message doesn't align with their ambitions.

It feels safe and comfortable. But you're fighting the current every day. Move to where the city is telling you to do what you want to do anyway.

## Quality of Life vs. Ambition

There's often a tradeoff between:
- Cities with strong ambition messages (high pressure, expensive, intense)
- Livable cities with good quality of life (more relaxed, less pressure)

**Neither is objectively better.** It depends on what you want. But be honest with yourself about what you're optimizing for.

## The Test

Here's a test: **imagine you have a project you're excited about. In which city would you want to work on it?**

The answer reveals what kind of ambient message you want. That's the city you should live in.

---

**Core Takeaway**: Cities send ambient messages about what kind of ambition matters. New York says "get rich," Cambridge says "be smart," Silicon Valley says "build important things." Choose a city whose message aligns with your goals—you'll be hearing it every day.
