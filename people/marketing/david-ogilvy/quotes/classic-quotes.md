# David Ogilvy 经典语录集

> 广告之父的智慧箴言 — 关于广告、营销、创意和商业的永恒洞察

---

## 📝 关于广告的本质

> "The consumer is not a moron. She is your wife."
> — Confessions of an Advertising Man

> "If it doesn't sell, it isn't creative."
> — On creativity in advertising

> "Nobody was ever bored into buying a product."
> — On engaging content

> "What really decides consumers to buy or not to buy is the content of your advertising, not its form."
> — On substance over style

> "The best ideas come as jokes. Make your thinking as funny as possible."
> — On creative thinking

> "I don't know the rules of grammar. If you're trying to persuade people to do something, it seems to me you should use their language."
> — On copywriting

> "Never write an advertisement which you wouldn't want your family to read. You wouldn't tell lies to your own wife. Don't tell them to mine."
> — On honesty in advertising

---

## 📊 关于研究

> "Advertising people who ignore research are as dangerous as generals who ignore decodes of enemy signals."
> — On the importance of research

> "The most important word in advertising is TEST. Test your product. Test your media. Test your headlines."
> — On systematic testing

> "I have never written an advertisement in the office. Too many interruptions. I do all my writing at home."
> — On his creative process

> "I spend a long time studying the precedents. I look at every advertisement which has appeared for competing products during the past 20 years."
> — On research habits

> "The trouble with market research is that people don't think what they feel, they don't say what they think, and they don't do what they say."
> — On research limitations

---

## 💡 关于创意

> "Unless your campaign is built on a Big Idea, it will pass like a ship in the night."
> — On Big Ideas

> "Big ideas are usually simple ideas."
> — On simplicity

> "You can recognize a Big Idea by the reaction of the client. He will be scared."
> — On identifying Big Ideas

> "In the modern world of business, it is useless to be a creative, original thinker unless you can also sell what you create."
> — On commercial creativity

> "If you ever have the good fortune to create a great advertising campaign, you will soon see another agency steal it. This is irritating, but don't let it worry you; nobody has ever built a brand by imitating somebody else's advertising."
> — On originality

---

## ✍️ 关于文案写作

> "On average, five times as many people read the headline as read the body copy."
> — On headlines

> "The more you tell, the more you sell."
> — On long copy

> "Do not address your readers as though they were gathered together in a stadium. When people read your copy, they are alone."
> — On intimate communication

> "Use short sentences. Use short first paragraphs. Use vigorous English. Be positive, not negative."
> — Writing rules

> "Write the way you talk. Naturally."
> — On natural language

> "Never use jargon words like reconceptualize, demassification, attitudinally, judgmentally. They are hallmarks of a pretentious ass."
> — On plain language

> "Good copy can't be written with tongue in cheek, written just for a living. You've got to believe in the product."
> — On authentic writing

---

## 🎯 关于标题

> "Avoid blind headlines—the kind which mean nothing unless you read the body copy underneath them; most people don't."
> — On clear headlines

> "It pays to include the brand name in the headline."
> — On brand visibility

> "Headlines with news are recalled by 22% more people than those without news."
> — On newsworthy headlines

> "When you advertise in a newspaper, you have to remember that your ad is competing with the news."
> — On competitive context

---

## 🏢 关于品牌建设

> "Every advertisement should be thought of as a contribution to the complex symbol which is the brand image."
> — On brand consistency

> "It takes a big idea to attract the attention of consumers and get them to buy your product. Unless your advertising contains a big idea, it will pass like a ship in the night."
> — On brand differentiation

> "Any damn fool can put on a deal, but it takes genius, faith, and perseverance to create a brand."
> — On brand building

> "The manufacturer who dedicates his advertising to building the most sharply defined personality for his brand gets the largest share of the market."
> — On brand personality

> "Repeat your winners. If you are lucky enough to write a good advertisement, repeat it until it stops selling. Scores of good advertisements have been discarded before they lost their potency."
> — On consistency

---

## 💼 关于客户服务

> "The most important word in the vocabulary of advertising is TEST."
> — On proving results

> "Ninety-nine percent of advertising doesn't sell much of anything."
> — On advertising effectiveness

> "Never stop testing, and your advertising will never stop improving."
> — On continuous improvement

> "First, make yourself a reputation for being a creative genius. Second, surround yourself with partners who are better than you are. Third, leave them to get on with it."
> — On building an agency

---

## 👔 关于领导力

> "Hire people who are better than you are, then leave them to get on with it. Look for people who will aim for the remarkable, who will not settle for the routine."
> — On hiring

> "I prefer the discipline of knowledge to the anarchy of ignorance."
> — On expertise

> "Talent, I believe, is most likely to be found among nonconformists, dissenters, and rebels."
> — On finding talent

> "I admire people with gentle manners who treat other people as human beings. I abhor quarrelsome people."
> — On workplace culture

> "Develop your eccentricities while you are young. That way, when you get old, people won't think you're going gaga."
> — Career advice

---

## 🎓 关于学习与成长

> "The pursuit of excellence is less profitable than the pursuit of bigness, but it can be more satisfying."
> — On values

> "I don't believe in tricky advertising. I don't believe in funny advertising. I don't believe in cute advertising. I just believe in advertising that sells."
> — On priorities

> "Set exorbitant standards, and give your people hell when they don't live up to them. There is nothing so demoralizing as a boss who tolerates second rate work."
> — On standards

> "If you always hire people who are smaller than you are, we shall become a company of dwarfs. If you always hire people who are bigger than you are, we shall become a company of giants."
> — On team building

---

## 📈 关于销售与结果

> "I do not regard advertising as entertainment or an art form, but as a medium of information."
> — On advertising purpose

> "Advertising is only evil when it advertises evil things."
> — On ethics

> "The business community wants remarkable advertising, but turns a cold shoulder to the kind of people who can produce it."
> — On creative recognition

> "Leaders grasp nettles."
> — On decision-making

---

## 🌟 关于成功

> "Play to win, but have fun doing it."
> — On work-life balance

> "It is useless to be a creative, original thinker unless you can also sell what you create."
> — On practical creativity

> "The secret of long life is double careers. One to about age sixty, then another for the next thirty years."
> — Life advice

> "I have a theory that the best ads come from personal experience. Some of the good ones I have done have really come out of the real experience of my life."
> — On authentic creativity

## 🎬 关于电视广告

> "Make the product the hero of the commercial."
> — On TV advertising

> "Use testimonials from real customers."
> — On credibility

> "Show the product in use."
> — On demonstration

> "Start with the gag. Open with fire."
> — On grabbing attention

## 🔮 关于未来

> "Much of the messy advertising you see on television today is the product of committees. Committees can criticize advertisements, but they should never be allowed to create them."
> — On creative process

> "The best way to get new clients is to do good work for your old clients."
> — On business development

> "A good advertisement is one which sells the product without drawing attention to itself."
> — On effective advertising
