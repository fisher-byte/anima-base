# Jackie Bavaro on product strategy and career growth

## Key Insights

### Getting into Product Management
- Cornell: Computer Science + Economics
- Never heard of PM role
- Friend convinced her to apply for PM internship
- Thought "I can't be a manager, I'm a sophomore!"
- Learned: "You manage the product, not the people"

### Microsoft Experience
- Interned on Microsoft SharePoint Services
- Loved the team, stayed full-time
- When moving to NY, Microsoft had no PM roles there

### Google Rejection → Motivation
- Applied to Google, got rejected
- Shocked: "I really had thought that I would pass the interview"
- Applied again a year later, got into Google APM program
- **Key insight**: "Even good PMs don't know how to answer these questions"
- Led to writing books to help others

### Writing the Books
- Constantly had people asking for interview help
- "My friend's applying to Google, can you talk to them?"
- Noticed patterns in interview responses:
  - Some people didn't understand what questions were trying to get at
  - Some sounded good but "their three ideas weren't actually good ideas"
- Wrote **Cracking the PM Interview** and **Cracking the PM Career**

### Asana Journey
- First PM at Asana
- Ended up as Head of Product Management
- Key focus: Product strategy

## Books Authored
1. **Cracking the PM Interview**
2. **Cracking the PM Career**
